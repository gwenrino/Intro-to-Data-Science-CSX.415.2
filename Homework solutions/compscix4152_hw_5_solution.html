The distributions are definitely not the same.

Corpus Christi stands out for having very little variability, while Fort Bend stands out as having a lot of variability.

Filtering out cities and months with fewer than 500 sales helps us narrow down our analysis to only those observations with a large amount of data. Smaller amounts of data means more uncertainty (variability). Also, depending on the problem, sometimes we simply dont care about certain subsets of the data. Perhaps, in this situation, we only care about major cities.

txhousing %>% rename(median_price = median) %>% filter(sales > 500) %>% ggplot() +
  geom_boxplot(aes(x = reorder(city, median_price, median), y = median_price)) +
